Nicole García Tubéns presented her first research in progress (RIP) talk for the [Human Medical Genetics and Genomics](https://www.cuanschutz.edu/graduate-programs/human-medical-genetics-and-genomics/home) PhD program.  She is a second year student who joined our lab in July 2025.  We are so proud of her work this far and looking foward to start of her thesis project.  If you are attending SIMD, make sure to come by Nicole's posters on ALDH7A1 variants and PDE-ALDH7A1 and the use of a mini gene to evalute variants of unknown significance in OTC deficiency.
